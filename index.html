About Me

Hello, I’m Jasmine!

I have recently finished my studies as a Digital Media Design student at Plymouth University. Technology has always fascinated me and my interest in digital design had driven me to study it. During my degree I completed modules on Web Design, Strategies for Digital Art and Technology, Animation, Virtual Reality and many other creative design subjects. This required me to utilise software from the Adobe Suite, Autodesk Maya and Unity to produce varying digital content. My portfolio includes a variety of examples including my art, animation and university work.

University Work

Below are examples of some of the projects I have created throughout my time at Plymouth University. Hover your mouse over the images to reveal more information. Some of projects also have more documentation and videos if you select the image title and follow the link.

Showreel

A horror themed showreel I created in my first year of university. I developed this using After Effects as a showcase of my skills.

Face Your Fears

This is my Final Year Project where I created a virtual reality simulation in which a person can become immersed in an environment where reality is exaggerated to simulate the subjective experiences of claustrophobia.

Dissertation

My dissertation on The Creative Potential of Virtual Reality in the Treatment of Phobias This study aims to examine the creative process of using virtual reality as a form of phobia treatment from the perspective of the psychology, technology and design principles.

Fireflies

Fireflies is a visually dynamic installation that focuses on immersive interaction through light and sound. The audience experiences a feeling of playing with light as they are exposed to the characteristics of the projected glowing fireflies which are influenced by the way the user interacts with the Kinect system.

Creative Coding

This is an interactive application created using three.js. The user can look around a 3D environment using their mouse and visualise it depending on the time of day. When the mouse is clicked it cycles through the time of day, changing the appearance of the environment.

Pawpoint

A web and mobile application that links to a light-up dog tracking collar – made using Arduino technology. Using this website the user can track their dogs location, set up a dog profile, create a virtual fence and change the light colours on the collar.
